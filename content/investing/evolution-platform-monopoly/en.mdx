Evolution is often bucketed with the rest of online gambling and priced at the sector's discount. That framing misses what the company actually is: a platform provider with software-like margins, contracts denominated in the operators' take rates, and an install base that grows every time a new studio comes online.

## The scale that keeps compounding

Every incremental studio hour is close to fixed cost, and every new jurisdiction that regulates online play tends to hand the addressable market a step change. The company doesn't need to win share to grow — it wins by the market normalizing around live products it has already built.

## What could go wrong

Regulatory risk in a single large market (US state-by-state, or a rewrite of the UK rules) would compress multiples faster than earnings. The stock also trades on the perception of AGS-style commoditization; if a competitor's studio hours ever start to feel like Evolution's, that thesis dissolves.

For now, neither risk looks priced in beyond the sector-wide discount, and the base case remains a mid-teens compounder trading at a mid-teens multiple.
