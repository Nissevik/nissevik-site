The interesting thing about Assa Abloy isn't the door hardware. It's the compounding — the same story you could have written in 2004, and 2014, and probably 2034. Bolt-on acquisitions of small, cash-generative product lines, financed at a cost of capital that most competitors can't touch.

## Where we are in the cycle

Renovation activity in North America and Europe has been depressed for two years. Most of the sell-side model that flat as the new normal. It isn't. When the cycle turns — even mildly — the mix shift toward higher-margin electronic access products lifts the entire earnings base.

## Why now

The stock has traded sideways for eighteen months while the business kept quietly executing. The multiple has done more work than the fundamentals, and for the first time since 2020, the compounding thesis is being offered at close to its own historical average — not at a premium.

The base case is that the next five years look a lot like the last twenty. That's not a headline, but it's often the right kind of investment.
